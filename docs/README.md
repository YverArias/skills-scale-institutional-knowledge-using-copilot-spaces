# OctoAcme Project Management Overview

OctoAcme’s project management processes are designed to provide structure and clarity from the initiation of a project through to its retrospective and continuous improvement. The approach begins with a strong foundation in project initiation, requiring a clear articulation of business need, measurable outcomes, and key stakeholders, captured in a Project One-pager. Once approved, planning moves forward with a kickoff meeting, backlog creation, estimation, and risk management. Each project cycles through a defined lifecycle: initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective, ensuring that critical deliverables and checkpoints are met along the way.

Central to successful execution are clearly defined roles and responsibilities. OctoAcme utilizes cross-functional teams comprised of Developers, Product Managers, and Project Managers, each with explicit goals. Developers focus on delivery, reliability, and maintainability while collaborating on planning and risk identification. Product Managers own the product vision, set priorities, and validate solutions, while Project Managers coordinate schedules, manage risks, facilitate meetings, and maintain documentation. These personas communicate through daily standups, weekly syncs, roadmap updates, and regular stakeholder briefings, ensuring transparency at every stage.

Key workflows emphasize iterative delivery with small, testable increments tracked using project boards (e.g., GitHub Projects) and well-defined stages such as Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, and Done. The pull request (PR) workflow enforces best practices: limiting PR size, linking related issues, using acceptance criteria, and requiring approvals before merge. Each phase is supported by checklists—such as for planning and execution—to reinforce consistency and accountability.

Quality assurance is embedded throughout the process. Rigorous CI practices enforce automated testing, security scanning, and linting for all PRs. Additional quality gates include manual QA for feature acceptance, end-to-end smoke tests for releases, and tracking of key performance metrics like velocity and burndown. Blockers and risks are systematically surfaced, triaged, and escalated as required, while regular retrospectives capture learnings to drive iterative improvement. Communication is continuous and adapted to the needs of each stakeholder group, leveraging templates, dashboards, and dedicated channels to provide status updates, incident communication, and escalation paths. This holistic approach ensures projects are delivered efficiently, with high quality, and with all team members aligned.
